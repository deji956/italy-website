The Colosseum

üìç Piazza del Colosseo, Rome, Italy
‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ (4.8/5 based on 45,230 reviews)

About This Attraction

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is the largest ancient amphitheater ever built and remains the largest standing amphitheater in the world today. Completed in 80 AD under Emperor Titus, this architectural marvel could hold between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators.

For over 400 years, the Colosseum hosted gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, executions, and dramatic reenactments of famous battles. Today, it stands as an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

The structure's innovative design included a complex system of vaults, underground passages (hypogeum), and trap doors that allowed for elaborate stage effects. Its influence on modern stadium architecture is still evident today.

About This Attraction

The Vatican Museums house one of the world's greatest art collections, assembled by popes over the centuries. With over 70,000 works displayed across 54 galleries, the museums showcase masterpieces spanning ancient Roman sculpture to Renaissance paintings.

The crown jewel is the Sistine Chapel, where Michelangelo's breathtaking ceiling frescoes and "Last Judgment" leave visitors speechless. The museums also feature Raphael's Rooms, the Gallery of Maps, and countless treasures from Egyptian, Etruscan, and classical antiquity.

The Amalfi Coast is a 50-kilometer stretch of coastline that's been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This full-day tour takes you along one of the world's most scenic drives, visiting the picturesque towns of Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello.

Experience dramatic clifftop views, pastel-colored houses cascading down to the sea, medieval architecture, and the sparkling Mediterranean. Enjoy free time to explore charming streets, shop for limoncello and local ceramics, and savor authentic Italian cuisine.
